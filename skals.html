Skals Å

Explore the tranquil Skals stream, a haven for bird-watchers. This serene walk offers the soothing sounds of flowing water and sights of birds gracefully gliding and nesting among the reeds.

Getting there

Park on the small small parking lot located at the start of the trail, coming out just south of Skals, on Løgstørvej 29. Here you will find the trail running along the stream and you will have to go back the same way to get back to the car.

Alternatively, Start in the town of Skals and walk along the path Himmerlandsstien until you reach the railroad bridge, and from there on you can walk the 2.2 km trail the other way round.

Flora and fauna

Walking along this nature trail, you'll encounter a diverse array of bird species, so keep an ear out for birds such as the the bluethroat, and If you're observant, you might even catch sight of it nestled in the dense reed forests.

Additionally, attentive listeners can be lucky to hear songs from the reed warbler, sedge warbler, reed bunting, and wren. Alongside these smaller songbirds, the trail is also home to larger avian inhabitants, some of which you may almost definitely find by the railroad bridge.

It's common to spot swans, stealthy herons, cormorants, and redshanks. The skies and waters here are often graced by the presence of lapwings, ducks, and geese, making for a rich birdwatching experience.
